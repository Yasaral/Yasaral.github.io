About

Hi, I'm Ali — a robotics and mechatronics engineer and PhD candidate at Monash University, where I work on force/torque control, hardware-in-the-loop testing, and mechanism design. I love working with real hardware and control problems. I'm currently working on agricultural robotics for crop harvesting, and during my master's I worked on force-controlled robots and physical human–robot interaction. Before my PhD, I worked in industry developing exoskeletons.

Selected Work

Soft Membrane–Fin-Ray Gripper

Compliant design · Hyperelastic FEA · Grasping

▶ demo video — add YouTube embed

A compliant gripper combining a pressurised membrane with Fin-Ray fingers for low-damage handling. Operating at 0–30 kPa, it increased contact area by 40% and achieved a 90% grasp success rate on spherical produce. Analytical deformation models were validated against hyperelastic FEA in ANSYS Workbench.

2-DOF Cable-Driven Robotic Wrist

Impedance control · EtherCAT · Real-time

▶ demo video — add YouTube embed

A cable-driven wrist for safe interaction in cluttered environments, built on a real-time stack of Maxon drives, EtherCAT, Beckhoff IPC, and ATI force/torque sensors. Impedance-based force control achieved under 1.5 N RMS tracking error at 250 Hz, staying stable under external loads up to 35 N.
